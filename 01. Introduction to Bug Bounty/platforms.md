# 🏆 Top Bug Bounty Platforms

## Major Platforms

### 💰 HackerOne
- **URL**: [hackerone.com](https://hackerone.com)
- **Founded**: 2012
- **Stats**: 3,000+ programs, $300M+ paid out
- **Best For**: Beginners and experienced hunters
- **Highlights**:
  - Hacker101 free training
  - Public and private programs
  - CTF competitions
  - Reputation system (H1 ranking)

### 🔒 Bugcrowd
- **URL**: [bugcrowd.com](https://bugcrowd.com)
- **Founded**: 2012
- **Best For**: Broad range of program types
- **Highlights**:
  - University program for students
  - Crowdcontrol (in-house scanning)
  - VDP and paid bounty programs
  - Skills assessments

### 🌐 Intigriti
- **URL**: [intigriti.com](https://intigriti.com)
- **Best For**: European programs
- **Highlights**:
  - Fast triage (avg. 24 hours)
  - Strong EU program base
  - Community events and challenges

### ⚡ Synack
- **URL**: [synack.com](https://synack.com)
- **Type**: Vetted / Private
- **Best For**: Experienced professionals
- **Highlights**:
  - Application required
  - Higher average payouts
  - Enterprise clients
  - Structured test methodology

### 🔍 YesWeHack
- **URL**: [yeswehack.com](https://yeswehack.com)
- **Best For**: European and global programs
- **Highlights**:
  - Growing platform
  - GDPR compliant
  - Hunter-friendly policies

## Choosing the Right Program as a Beginner

### Criteria for Beginners
1. **Public program** — anyone can participate
2. **Broad scope** — more targets to find bugs in
3. **Active triage** — quick responses
4. **Fair policy** — no aggressive duplicate policy
5. **Good reputation** — check hacker reviews

### Recommended First Programs
- Companies with wide web app scope
- Programs with "safe harbor" policies
- Programs offering reputation rewards even for low severity
- Programs that explicitly welcome new researchers

## Tracking Your Progress

Use spreadsheets to track:
- Programs tested
- Vulnerabilities found (and their status)
- Earnings per month
- Time invested vs. reward
- Skills learned
